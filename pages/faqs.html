Frequently Asked Questions

An apprenticeship is a paid job that combines practical work experience with structured training. Apprentices gain industry-recognised qualifications while working and earning a wage.

You can contact support via email at support@future.com, fill out our contact form here or call us at 123-456-7890.

Apprenticeships are open to anyone aged 16 or over, living in the UK. There is no upper age limit, and they are available to school leavers, career changers, and those looking to upskill.

  • Level 2 (Intermediate): Equivalent to GCSEs; entry-level roles.
  • Level 3 (Advanced): Equivalent to A-levels; builds specialist skills.
  • Level 4-5 (Higher): Equivalent to foundation degreeand above, these are higher apprenticeships.
  • Level 6 (Degree): Equivalent to a full bachelor's degree.
  • Level 7 (Master/’s): Equivalent to a master's degree; senior-level training.

An apprenticeship allows you to earn while you learn. This means you can put your knowledge straight into practice whilst gaining invaluable experience. It's a great way to start a career, whilst being debt free!

The length of a degree apprenticeship depends on the level and type. Most degree apprenticeships last around 3-6 years. You can find out about the duration of a specific apprenticeship on their job description page.

Your company may decide they would like to keep you on and therefore you can continue working for them. However, you may decide to move on to different opportunities.
